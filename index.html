Global Warming In Singapore

What is Global Warming?

Global warming is the long-term heating of Earth’s surface observed since the pre-industrial period (between 1850 and 1900) due to human activities, primarily fossil fuel burning, which increases heat-trapping greenhouse gas levels in Earth’s atmosphere. (NASA, 2024)

Impact of Climate Change on Singapore

  • From 1984 to 2022, the annual mean temperature rose by 0.24°C per decade.(NCCS, 2023)
  • Rainfall has increased in recent years. Annual rainfall total for Singapore has been gradually increasing at a rate of 83 mm per decade from 1980 to 2022. (NCCS, 2023)
    As a low-lying island, the rise in sea level poses the most immediate threat to Singapore. Much of our nation lies only 15 m above the Singapore Height Datum, with about 30 per cent of our main island being less than 5 m above the Singapore Height Datum.
  • The mean sea level around Singapore is observed to be increasing at a rate of around 3.5mm per year in the period from 1993 to 2021. (NCCS, 2023)
